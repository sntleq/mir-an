Mir-an is a language spoken in the old times by The People of Mir. They call themselves Miro.

They live in yes, you guessed it right, Mir, an entire continent presently divided into eight regions, one being the capital of course, Zanmir. The name just literally says "middle land" because yes, you guessed it right again, it's the middle region. As the political system glides through time, the leader always gets called the Dao.

Mir-an literally means "language of Mir", that simple. As said earlier, Mir-an was spoken in the old times. I'm not yet sure if it will be used as frequently in the modern times. It's because the language is supposed to be simple, though not as simple as Toki Pona, but pretty sure it will be a bit hard to express modern concepts. I'm still working on very basic concepts and making sure words are short as possible to make room for more complex ones. This, I think, is the reason of my slow progress, when I make a word, I want it to be one-syllable only so I don't end up having long words when I start smashing them. is this a real thing? longwordophobia?

But anyways, despite the probably less usage of the language in the modern times, Mir-an will surely be used in maybe old traditional literature like poems. The people might even revive it if it actually dies like Latin or Hebrew. Pretty sure there is at least one Miro who speaks it here in Oar (Earth) ;) I just want somewhere to run to when the world just hate the entirety of me. Mir, it is :) ps: im fine :)

Mircho, the writing system, is the reason why I started all of this. Transliterating English to it looks terrible so I decided to make a conlang for it. This is also why Mir-an is set in the old times. I'll discuss how it works in the orthography section.
