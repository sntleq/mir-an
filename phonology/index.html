PHONOTACTICS

Mir-an's phonotactical constraint is a simple (C)V(m, n, r).

All consonants in the inventory are allowed as onsets, but only m, n, and r are allowed to end syllables as codas. Consonant clusters and geminates are not allowed.

Permitted diphthongs and triphthongs are /ja/, /ji/, /jo/, /wa/, /wi/, /wo/, /aj/, /aw/, /jaw/ and /waj/.

Consecutive syllables should not have the same consonant as their onsets. ie. nonam For this, other consonants can take the place of some consonants: [ɾ] for /d/, and [z] for /s/ There is also a similar thing for vowels, Mir-an tends to avoid the same vowel surrounding a consonant ie. ama, but /o/ is an exception, ie. iozonobor

ALLOPHONY

Plosives are voiced intervocalically. ie. bor /por/ but uozobor /wozʊbor/ This may(?) happen with fricatives and the affricate too, but have not yet so far. For the case of /s/ and /z/ though, they're different phonemes so /z/ can also occur in the position of /s/ (not intervocalic position). Using the two can alter the meaning a bit. sar and zar both means "light", but the former is in the physical and literal context, and the latter is in the more figurative and philosophical context. does this all even make sense? In cases where the same consonant can't surround a vowel/diphthong/rime, it's like /s/ and /z/, and /d/ and /ɾ/ are in free variation. makes sense, no? Lastly, /o/ raises to [ʊ] in unstressed syllables.
