PHONOTACTICS

Mir-an's phonotactical constraint is very simple.

(C)V(m, n, r)

All consonants in the inventory are allowed as onsets, but only /m/, /n/, and /r/ are allowed to end syllables as codas. Consonant clusters and geminates are not allowed.

Permitted diphthongs and triphthongs are:

/ja/, /ji/, /jo/, /wa/, /wi/, /wo/, /aj/, /aw/, /jaw/ and /waj/

Consecutive syllables should not have the same consonant as their onsets.

ie. nonam

For this, they'll have to dissimilate, other consonants can take the place of some consonants: [ɾ] for /d/, and [z] for /s/

There is also a similar thing for vowels, Mir-an tends to avoid the same vowel surrounding a consonant

ie. ama

but /o/ is an exception, ie. iozonobor. iz fine?

ALLOPHONY

Obstruents are voiced intervocalically.

ie. bor /por/ but uozobor /wozʊbor/

For the case of /s/ and /z/ though, they're different phonemes, /z/ can also occur in the position of /s/ (not intervocalic position). Substituting one on another can alter the meaning of a word. even just a bit :).

sar and zar both means "light", but the former is in the physical and literal context, and the latter is in the more figurative and philosophical context. im not sure if this is a good example but i just know that /z/ isn't just an allophone of /s/. i think of it as a different phoneme. pls tell me tho if the given example is indeed a pair different words, or if they're actually the same, just used in different context.

In cases where the same consonant can't surround a vowel/diphthong/rime, it's like /s/ and /z/, and /d/ and /ɾ/ are in free variation. makes sense, no? Lastly, /o/ raises to [ʊ] in unstressed syllables.
